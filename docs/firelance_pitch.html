Firelance

A squad-based multiplayer game where teams build temporary keeps, grow rich through kills and objectives, then risk everything on dangerous banking runs before rival squads crack the walls and steal the vault.

Think old-school top-down PvP projectile chaos with Age of Empires readability and a modern competitive sheen — less cartoony, more torchlight, steel, mud, banners, and bad decisions.

The core hook

The richer you get, the more the world wants you dead.

Firelance is not just about winning fights. It is about becoming valuable, deciding when to cash out, and surviving the obvious consequences of greed.

Build a keep. Fill the vault. Escort the gold. Try not to become a public service announcement.

Every squad starts scattered, chooses a defensible location, builds a keep, and uses it as both respawn point and vault. Gold earned through kills and objectives goes into the keep first. To actually win, the squad must physically move that gold to neutral towns and bank it under pressure.

01 / Claim

Pick the keep site

Terrain is known, but enemies are hidden by fog of war. Squads race toward hills, ruins, forests, and chokepoints — and may collide before the first wall goes up.

02 / Grow

Kill, assist, build, scout

Kills and objectives feed gold into the keep. Personal bounty rises as players perform, making them more profitable and more dangerous to carry.

03 / Cash out

Bank or get plundered

Gold in the keep is only potential victory. Banked gold is safe. Big banking runs leave the keep vulnerable and turn the carrier into walking bait.

Look + feel

Readable strategy, gritty action, premium sheen.

The camera and clarity should feel tactical. The surface treatment should feel modern: lit armor edges, projectile trails, foggy forests, firelight, readable silhouettes, and sharp UI.

Top-down tactical viewWide enough to read formations, projectiles, walls, and flank routes. Close enough that a single arrow still feels personal.
Dodgeable projectile combatArrows, bolts, javelins, bombs, and low-fantasy magic are visible objects. Aim, lead, dodge, block, and use terrain.
Medieval, but not dustyAge-of-Empires legibility with more dramatic lighting, cleaner UI, richer materials, and darker competitive mood.
Less cartoon, more steelStylized realism: readable silhouettes, tasteful effects, no neon spell soup, no comedy-proportioned shoulder pads unless we are punishing someone.
Core systems

Simple rules that create ugly, beautiful choices.

The systems are designed to produce stories: escort missions, third-party raids, emergency rebuilds, bait banking, desperate defenses, and high-bounty executions.

Keep = respawn + vault

If the keep stands, dead players respawn. If it falls, respawns stop and the vault spills into lootable sacks and chests.

Gold starts unsafe

Kills and objectives send gold to the squad keep first. It only becomes victory score after a successful town banking run.

Banking is physical

Gold becomes a carried strongbox. Bigger deposits are slower, louder, and more tempting to ambush.

Everyone builds

All players can build and repair basic structures. Engineers specialize in gates, towers, traps, and advanced defenses.

Bounty adds pressure

Higher bounty increases reward and visibility. It does not simply make the player stronger. Success paints the target.

Fog hides intent

Terrain is known, but keeps, traps, banking runs, and enemy movement must be scouted or inferred from rumors.

MVP scope

Build the smallest version that proves the panic.

The first playable should avoid procedural map generators, sprawling tech trees, and survival-game feature tar pits. Prove bounty, banking, keeps, and plunder first.

Players4 squads of 3 players. Enough chaos for third-party raids without needing a massive population.
MapOne static forest/ruins map with randomized spawns, 8–12 legal keep sites, 2–3 towns, and fog of war.
ClassesFighter, Ranger, Engineer. Clear roles, readable counters, and enough variety for team decisions.
StructuresKeep, wall, gate, tower, trap. Hard caps prevent cursed medieval suburbia.
CombatTop-down movement, mouse aim, visible projectiles, melee arcs, shields, dodges, and simple hazards.
GoldKill and objective gold goes to the keep. Banked gold wins. Destroyed keeps spill unbanked gold.
BountyKills, assists, survival, and banking raise personal bounty. Higher bounty means higher reward and tracking pressure.
Match25–35 minutes. Surviving squad with the most banked gold wins. Exile/rebuild rules tested later.
First dev slice

One playable slice, no feature goblin infestation.

The first prototype should answer one question: is it fun when squads fight over a keep, a bounty target, and a banking run?

Combat sandboxTop-down movement, mouse aim, slow projectiles, simple health, death, respawn, and one compact test arena.
Pre-placed keepsSkip building temporarily. Let each squad spawn at a basic keep that stores gold and controls respawns.
Bounty + kill goldKills and assists raise bounty. Kill rewards go to the squad keep. High-bounty kills generate meaningful swings.
Banking runTurn vault gold into a carried strongbox, move it to town, channel to bank, and make it safe victory score.
Keep destructionAttacking a keep disables respawn pressure and spills loot into sacks/chests that anyone can steal.
Basic buildingAdd walls, gates, towers, traps, and the Engineer only after bounty + banking already feel good.

The brother sell

This is the old addictive top-down PvP feeling, but rebuilt around one modern problem: what happens when the best player becomes the biggest payday on the map?

The keeps create territory. The bounty creates obsession. The banking run creates the bad decision. That is the whole toy box.
